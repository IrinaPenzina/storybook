Eclectic

People would ask me “what kind of music do you like?”, expecting to hear a handful of genres as a response, so that maybe, they can put me in a box or confirm that we belong together. My answer would always be something annoying. One word. Eclectic. I like, or rather can appreciate any type of music. That’s the real answer to that question, but actually the full story is more complex and deeper too.
Answering that my music tastes are eclectic is my way of saying that music for me is like the original soundtrack of a movie. It’s not background, ambiant or an afterthought; it is an element that transcends, dramatizes and sublimes the unfolding action. A bit like how Quentin Tarantino (did I say I was a fan?) chooses opening or closing credit songs for his movies.
The same way that I quite enjoy annoying (some) people with my single worded answer, I like to imagine that the AI in charge of of suggesting music for me on online platforms is equally disgruntled when I seemingly do a 180 degrees sharp turn from gansta rap to 17th century classical baroque; pulling its digital hair when realizing it once more has do to the learning all over again.
